Soccer Computer Vision Project

  • Project Intro
    • Applies computer vision and unsupervised learning to professional soccer match footage.
    • Uses Roboflow and Hugging Face APIs to detect players and the ball.
    • Projects camera views onto a normalized pitch using homography.
  • Overview / Building Process
    • Ingested annotated match footage via the Roboflow API.
    • Extracted frame-level positional data for players and ball.
    • Applied homography transformations for spatial normalization.
    • Used K-means clustering to analyze player positioning patterns.
    • Constructed Voronoi diagrams to model zones of control.
    • Visualized ball trajectories to analyze tempo and movement.
  • Outcome
    • Converted raw broadcast footage into spatially consistent tactical analytics.
  • Results
    • Tracked players and ball across video frames.
    • Generated homography-aligned pitch mappings.
    • Visualized player influence via Voronoi diagrams.
